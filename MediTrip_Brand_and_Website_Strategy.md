# MediTrip — Brand & Website Strategy

**Domain:** meditourdworld.com  ·  **Brand name:** MediTrip
**Business:** Medical-treatment visa, abroad tour planning, guiding, ticketing & related travel services
**Phase 1 destination market:** China  ·  **Document date:** June 2026
**Prepared as:** Founder's working strategy (brand + website) based on deep research of competitors

---

## 1. Executive summary

MediTrip is a one-stop medical-travel facilitator that takes a Bangladeshi patient from "I need treatment abroad" to "I'm home and recovered" — handling the medical visa, hospital appointment, air ticket, accommodation, airport transfer, interpreter and follow-up. We launch with **China** as the flagship destination, where treatment costs run **50–70% below** Western prices, surgical volume is among the highest in the world, and new visa facilitation makes entry easier than ever.

The market is real and already served: **GD Assist** (Green Delta) is the established Bangladeshi leader, and **ChinaTravelMed** shows what a polished, China-specific facilitator looks like. Neither owns the "Bangladesh → China, done properly, in your language" position. That is MediTrip's opening.

This document covers the brand (the MediTrip logo system), what competitors do and where the gaps are, and a complete, build-ready website plan: sitemap, page-by-page content, design system, trust signals, and a conversion strategy. A working HTML homepage mockup accompanies it.

---

## 2. The opportunity

**Why patients leave Bangladesh.** Long waits, limited specialist capacity, and uncertainty about diagnosis push tens of thousands of Bangladeshis abroad every year for treatment — historically to India, Thailand, Singapore and Turkey. The decision is stressful, the logistics (visa, appointment, money transfer, language) are the hardest part, and patients overwhelmingly choose an agency they *trust* over the cheapest option.

**Why China, why now.**

- **Cost:** Most procedures cost 50–70% less than North America/Europe and are competitive with India while offering higher surgical volumes.
- **Capability:** Chinese surgeons in oncology, orthopedics, cardiac and transplant perform far more procedures annually than Western counterparts — volume drives outcomes. JCI-accredited and national Class III (三甲) hospitals offer international patient departments.
- **A different paradigm:** Integrated Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) for chronic and post-treatment recovery is something no other destination markets credibly.
- **Access:** China's expanding visa-facilitation and hospital invitation-letter system makes the medical visa pathway faster than it used to be.
- **White space:** Indian and Thai medical tourism from Bangladesh is crowded. A *China specialist* serving Bangladeshi patients in Bangla is differentiated.

**The core promise we are selling is not "cheap." It is "handled."** The patient's real fear is being alone, sick, and lost in a foreign hospital. MediTrip removes that fear.

## 3. Competitive landscape

### 3.1 GD Assist (gdassist.com) — Bangladesh market leader
Backed by Green Delta Insurance; a decade-plus operating history. Positions as **"Global Healthcare, Local Support"** and **"One-Stop Medical & Travel Solutions."**

What they do well:
- Broad service stack: medical tourism, **air & ground ambulance**, **health card** (discounts), pharmacy (Aster), second medical opinion, telemedicine, travel packages, air ticket & visa, and **TPA** (insurance claim administration).
- Heavy, credible **trust signals**: ISO 9001:2015, Superbrands, Diplomatic Council; authorized by **ATAB, IATA, Civil Aviation Authority**; named-client testimonials (barristers, MDs); animated stat counters (countries / clients / hospitals / years).
- Multiple physical offices (Dhaka, Chattogram), a **hotline (16457)**, and an embedded WhatsApp chat widget.
- A single homepage lead form with a service picker.

Where they leave room:
- Generalist across many destinations — **no deep China specialism**; China is one of many.
- Corporate/insurance-led tone; less of a warm, patient-story-driven experience.
- Template-built (Avada/WooCommerce) look; design is functional, not distinctive.

### 3.2 ChinaTravelMed (chinatravelmed.com) — China-specialist benchmark
A Beijing-based facilitator serving 40+ countries. This is the **structural template** to learn from for a China-focused site.

What they do well:
- Sharp positioning: **"Heal better, spend less in China"**, with three pillars — **Proven Quality** (JCI / Class III), **Significant Savings** (50–70%), **Destination Appeal** (recovery as travel).
- A persuasive **"Why fly to China"** narrative: home country = expensive/slow/no solution → China = volume + TCM + speed → focused 2–8 week treatment window.
- **Condition-led content** ("5 conditions where China leads") naming real hospitals (Peking Union, West China Hospital, Guang'anmen, Cancer Hospital CAMS) and realistic timelines — this builds authority and serves search intent.
- Hospital & doctor cards (accreditation, languages spoken, ratings), a **travel & visa concierge** block (visa-free updates, 7-day invitation letters, airport fast-track), an SEO blog, verified testimonials, multilingual (EN/中文/ES), and a **"free assessment / roadmap within 24 hours"** CTA.

Where they leave room:
- Built for an international, mostly Western/Middle-Eastern audience — **not Bangladesh-specific** (no Bangla, no Dhaka office, no BDT, no local trust cues).
- Some modules are placeholders ("prepared for future release").

### 3.3 Other reference players
Medigence, MedChina, Better/MTA, BAC Medical Tourism (international China facilitators); Susthotaa, Meherba, Thai Health Care BD, Karetrip (Bangladesh/India-Thailand outbound). Common pattern across all: **visa assistance + appointment + ticket + accommodation + interpreter + follow-up**, sold through a free-quote lead form and WhatsApp.

### 3.4 What this means for MediTrip
| Lever | Market standard | MediTrip's move |
|---|---|---|
| Positioning | Generalist OR China-for-the-world | **China specialist, for Bangladesh, in Bangla** |
| Trust | Certifications + counters + testimonials | Same, plus **patient video stories** & transparent pricing |
| Content | Service lists | **Condition + hospital led** authority content |
| Language | English (or English+Chinese) | **Bangla + English** (add 中文 later) |
| Conversion | Quote form + WhatsApp | **WhatsApp-first + free assessment in 24h** |
| Money | Rarely addressed | **BDT pricing, payment & remittance guidance** |

## 4. The MediTrip brand

### 4.1 Name & idea
**MediTrip** = *Medi* (medical, care, safety) + *Trip* (travel, journey, movement). The wordmark literally fuses the two halves of the business. The reading is instant and works in both Bangla- and English-speaking contexts.

### 4.2 The logo system (10 samples delivered)
The signature device is built into the two letter "i"s:
- The dot of the **"i" in *Medi*** becomes a **medical sign** (a cross / plus, a care-heart, or a heartbeat pulse depending on the variant).
- The dot of the **"i" in *Trip*** becomes an **airplane pointing upward** — departure, ascent, the journey beginning.

Ten variations are provided so you can choose a direction: a primary horizontal lockup, a gradient version, a circular emblem/badge, a single-colour version for documents, a dark-mode version, a clinical pulse+jet version, a warm heart version, a global globe+plane version, a thin premium version, and an app/favicon icon. Recommended primary: **`01_primary`** (teal *Medi* + navy *Trip*, red medical cross, coral plane) for its clarity and instant medical-travel read; **`10_appicon`** as the app/social avatar.

### 4.3 Colour
- **Medical Teal `#0FA39A`** — primary; trust, health, calm.
- **Deep Navy `#15314F`** — authority, stability, premium.
- **Coral `#FF6B5E`** — energy, the journey, CTA accents.
- **Medical Red `#E5484D`** — reserved for the cross / urgent care cues.
- Neutrals: Ink `#102A43`, Slate `#33475B`, Cloud `#F4F7FA`, White.

Teal + navy reads "clinical and trustworthy"; coral keeps it human and travel-warm rather than cold and hospital-like.

### 4.4 Typography
**Poppins** for headings and the wordmark (geometric, friendly, modern, renders well in Latin). Pair with a clean readable body face (Inter / system UI). For Bangla content use a quality Bangla web font (e.g. **Hind Siliguri** or **Noto Sans Bengali**) so the Bangla experience is first-class, not an afterthought.

## 5. Positioning & messaging

**Positioning statement:** *MediTrip is Bangladesh's China medical-travel specialist — we arrange world-class treatment in accredited Chinese hospitals at a fraction of the cost, and handle every step in your own language: visa, appointment, tickets, stay, interpreter and recovery.*

**Primary headline (homepage hero):** "World-class treatment in China — fully arranged, in your language."
**Supporting line:** "From medical visa to recovery, MediTrip handles your entire journey. Get a free treatment & cost assessment within 24 hours."

**Message pillars** (mirror ChinaTravelMed's proven three, localised):
1. **Trusted hospitals** — JCI / Class III (三甲) accredited, international patient departments, named specialists.
2. **Major savings** — 50–70% less than Western care; transparent, all-in BDT estimates.
3. **Everything handled** — visa, appointment, ticket, hotel, airport pickup, Bangla/Mandarin interpreter, follow-up.

## 6. Service offering (site + business)

Group services into two families, as GD Assist does, so the site can scale:

**Medical**
- Treatment planning & hospital/doctor matching (China first)
- Medical visa assistance + hospital invitation letter
- Free second medical opinion / treatment cost estimate
- On-ground interpreter & patient coordinator
- Teleconsultation / pre-travel video consult
- Post-treatment follow-up & TCM recovery programs

**Travel**
- Air ticketing
- Visa processing (medical & accompanying attendant)
- Hotel / serviced-apartment booking near hospitals
- Airport fast-track, pickup & local transport
- Travel insurance
- Recovery-friendly tour add-ons for patient & companion

A clear **"How it works"** in 4 steps anchors the offering: **1) Share reports → 2) Get hospital options + cost estimate (24h) → 3) We arrange visa, ticket & stay → 4) Travel, treat & recover with us beside you.**

## 7. Website plan

### 7.1 Sitemap
```
Home
About  (story, mission, team, certifications, why China)
Services
   ├─ Medical: Treatment Planning · Medical Visa · Second Opinion ·
   │           Interpreter · Teleconsultation · Follow-up & TCM
   └─ Travel:  Air Ticketing · Visa Processing · Hotels · Transport ·
               Insurance · Tour Add-ons
Destinations  →  China (hub page)  [India / Thailand / etc. "coming soon"]
Treatments    (Oncology, Orthopedics & Spine, Cardiac, Fertility/IVF,
               Dental, Diagnostics/Second Opinion, TCM Recovery)
Hospitals     (partner hospital + doctor cards)
How It Works
Packages / Get a Free Quote   (primary conversion page)
Resources / Blog              (guides, cost comparisons, visa updates)
Patient Stories               (text + video testimonials)
Contact                       (offices, hotline, WhatsApp, map, form)
```

### 7.2 Homepage section blueprint (the order that converts)
1. **Sticky header** — logo, nav, language toggle (BN/EN), phone + **WhatsApp** button, "Free Assessment" CTA.
2. **Hero** — headline + sub + two CTAs ("Get Free Assessment", "WhatsApp Us") + reassurance microcopy ("Reply within 24 hours · No obligation"). Warm image of a patient/family, not a sterile OR.
3. **Trust bar** — animated counters (Patients served · Partner hospitals · Years · Cities in China) + accreditation logos (JCI, Class III, ATAB, IATA as applicable).
4. **Why China** — the 3 pillars (Trusted hospitals · Major savings · Everything handled).
5. **How it works** — 4 steps with icons.
6. **Services** — Medical + Travel cards.
7. **Treatments where China leads** — condition cards (oncology, ortho, cardiac, IVF, dental, diagnostics, TCM) with indicative savings and timelines.
8. **Featured hospitals** — 2–3 partner cards (accreditation, specialties, languages).
9. **Cost comparison** — simple "China vs. home" snapshot for 2–3 common procedures (in BDT).
10. **Patient stories** — testimonial slider (aim for video).
11. **Free assessment form** — name, phone/WhatsApp, condition, reports upload, preferred timeline.
12. **Footer** — offices, hotline, WhatsApp, socials, certifications, quick links, privacy.

### 7.3 Content principles
- **Bangla-first, English-available.** Real bilingual copy, not machine-translated.
- **Show, don't claim:** name hospitals, show accreditations, give realistic timelines and BDT ranges.
- **Patient-anxiety-led copy:** answer "Is it safe? How much? Who helps me there? What about the language?" on every key page.
- **Compliance & honesty:** never guarantee medical outcomes; "indicative" pricing; clear consent and privacy for uploaded medical reports.

## 8. Design system (for build)

- **Layout:** generous whitespace, 1200px max content width, rounded cards (12–16px radius), soft shadows. Clinical but warm.
- **Colour use:** teal/navy structure, coral only for CTAs and accents, lots of white, pale teal section backgrounds.
- **Type scale:** Poppins 600/700 for headings; 16–18px body; strong hierarchy.
- **Components:** sticky nav, hero with dual CTA, counter strip, icon feature cards, treatment/hospital cards, step timeline, testimonial slider, sticky WhatsApp button, multi-field lead form.
- **Imagery:** real patients/families, Chinese hospital exteriors/skylines, Bangla-speaking coordinators. Avoid generic stethoscope stock.
- **Trust everywhere:** accreditation row in header/footer, certifications block, named testimonials, response-time promise.
- **Mobile-first:** most Bangladeshi traffic is mobile; WhatsApp + tap-to-call must be one thumb away.

## 9. Conversion & growth

- **WhatsApp-first.** Floating WhatsApp button on every page + click-to-chat in hero. It is the default channel for Bangladeshi customers.
- **"Free assessment in 24 hours"** as the single, repeated primary CTA.
- **Lead form with report upload** so the team can return a real hospital + cost estimate fast.
- **Trust accelerators:** verified patient stories (video > text), transparent BDT cost ranges, visible offices + hotline.
- **SEO content engine:** condition guides ("Cancer treatment cost in China from Bangladesh"), "China medical visa from Bangladesh" guide, hospital profiles, cost-comparison posts, visa-policy updates — exactly the content that ranks and that ChinaTravelMed uses well.
- **Channels:** Facebook/Instagram (where BD audience is), Google Search for high-intent terms, referral incentives, doctor/diagnostic-centre partnerships in Dhaka & Chattogram.

---

## 10. Build recommendations

- **Fast path:** WordPress + a quality theme (as GD Assist did) — quickest to launch, easy for non-developers to update, strong plugin ecosystem (forms, WhatsApp, multilingual via WPML/Polylang).
- **Custom path:** Next.js + headless CMS — better performance/SEO and a more distinctive design; needs a developer.
- **Must-haves at launch:** SSL, fast mobile performance, BN/EN toggle, WhatsApp + tap-to-call, lead form with secure file upload, Google Analytics + Search Console, Facebook Pixel, privacy policy + medical-data consent.
- Use the accompanying **HTML homepage mockup** as the design reference for whichever path you choose.

## 11. Suggested launch roadmap

1. **Brand lock:** pick the primary logo from the 10 samples; finalise colour + fonts (incl. Bangla font).
2. **Content:** write Bangla+English copy for Home, About, How It Works, China hub, 4–6 treatment pages, and 3 starter blog guides.
3. **Partners:** sign 2–4 Chinese hospitals / facilitator partners; collect accreditations, doctor bios, photos.
4. **Build:** homepage first (from the mockup), then service/treatment/hospital pages, then blog.
5. **Trust assets:** register with ATAB/IATA as applicable; gather first patient stories.
6. **Launch + market:** GA/Search Console, Facebook campaigns, WhatsApp business, doctor-referral outreach.
